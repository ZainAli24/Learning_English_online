# 1. ⏰ Time of Day (Simple Samajh)

## 🌅 Morning (Subah)

👉 6 AM – 12 PM
👉 Jab tum uthte ho

---

## ☀️ Noon (Dopahar ka exact beech)

👉 **12 PM (sirf ek time)**
👉 Din ka bilkul beech

---

## 🌤️ Afternoon (Noon ke baad ka time)

👉 **12 PM ke baad → shaam se pehle**

📌 Yani:

* Noon = 12 PM (sirf ek point)
* Afternoon = 12 ke baad ka pura time

👉 Approx:
**12 PM – 5 PM**

---

## 🌇 Evening (Shaam)

👉 5 PM – 7/8 PM

---

## 🌙 Night (Raat)

👉 8 PM ke baad

---

# 🧠 Easy Trick

👉 Noon = **sirf 12 baje**
👉 Afternoon = **12 ke baad wala time**

---

# 🔥 Example

* I eat lunch at noon. → 12 baje
* I study in the afternoon. → 12 ke baad

---

# 🎯 Final Simple Line

👉 **Afternoon = noon ke baad ka time (dopahar ka baqi hissa)**

# 3. “Let” & "Let`s"  kya hota hai?

👉 **Let = allow karna / karne dena**

---

# 🧠 Simple samajh:

👉 Jab hum kisi ko kehte hain:
**“karne do” ya “allow karo”**

---

# 📌 Examples:

* Let me go.
  👉 mujhe jane do

* Let him speak.
  👉 usay bolne do

---



# 📘 Let’s = Let us

👉 **Let’s** asal mein **“Let us”** ka short form hai

---

# 🧠 Simple matlab:

👉 **Let us = chalo / aao mil kar kuch karein**

---

# 🔥 Easy samajh:

👉 Jab tum kisi ko invite karte ho
👉 ya kehte ho “aao mil kar karein”
👉 to tum **Let’s** use karte ho

---

# 📌 Examples

* Let’s go.
  👉 chalo chalte hain

* Let’s eat.
  👉 chalo khana khate hain

* Let’s study.
  👉 chalo parhte hain

---

# ⚡ Easy Trick

👉 **Let’s = chalo + action**

---

# ❗ Important

👉 “Let’s” hamesha **do logon ke liye hota hai (you + me)**

---


# ⚡ Easy Difference

👉 **Let** = kisi ko allow karna
👉 **Let’s** = mil ke kuch karne ka suggestion

---


# 🎯 Final Simple Line

👉 **Let = karne do**
👉 **Let’s = chalo mil ke karein**

---

# 5. "Over time" ka matlab:

---

# 🧠 Simple samajh:

👉 Ek dum nahi
👉 thoda thoda karke
👉 time ke sath improvement hoti hai

---

# 🔥 Tumhara sentence:

**Over time, you will understand more and more.**

👉 Matlab:
**Waqt ke sath sath tum zyada aur zyada samajhne lago ge**

---

# 📌 Aur examples:

* Over time, I will improve my English.
  👉 dheere dheere meri English better ho jayegi

* Over time, he became strong.
  👉 waqt ke sath wo strong ho gaya

---

# ⚡ Easy Trick

👉 **Over time = slowly slowly + time ke sath**

---

# 🎯 Final Simple Line

👉 **Over time = waqt ke sath dheere dheere**

# 6. “Have” ka simple matlab

👉 **“Have” ka ek hi matlab nahi hota**
👉 situation ke hisaab se matlab change hota hai

---

# 🧠 Step 1: Do type yaad karo

## 🟢 1. Have = “mere paas hai”

👉 jab cheez ho

* I have a mobile.
  👉 mere paas mobile hai

* I have a book.
  👉 mere paas kitab hai

---

## 🔵 2. Have = “main karta hoon / leta hoon”

👉 jab **daily routine ya food** ho

* I have breakfast.
  👉 main nashta karta hoon

* I have lunch.
  👉 main lunch karta hoon

---

# ⚡ Ab main confusion clear karta hoon

👉 Tum soch rahe ho:

“I have breakfast” = mere paas breakfast hai ❌

👉 Lekin English mein:
👉 ye ek **fixed expression** hai

👉 iska matlab hota hai:
✔️ **main nashta karta hoon**

---

# 🧠 Easy trick (100% kaam karega)

👉 Sentence dekho:

### 🍔 Food ho?

👉 have = khana

* I have tea → main chai peeta hoon

---

### 📦 Cheez ho?

👉 have = paas hona

* I have a car → mere paas car hai

---

# 🔥 Ultra Simple Rule

👉 **Food → have = khana**
👉 **Object → have = paas hona**

---

# 🎯 Final Example

* I have breakfast in morning
  👉 main subah nashta karta hoon

## 7. On Meaning: 
#### "On" ka matlab Roman Urdu mein "par" (پر), "oopar" (اوپر), ya "kisi cheez ke mutaliq" (regarding) hota hai. Yeh position ya waqt batane ke liye istemaal hota hai. 

#### Misalein (Examples):
- The book is on the table: Kitab mez par (par/oopar) hai.
- On Monday: Peer ko/par (waqt ke liye).
- On request: Darkhwast par (mutaliq).

#### Context ke hisaab se iska Roman Urdu mein istemaal badal sakta hai, lekin aksar jagah "par" (پر) hi nikalta hai.
