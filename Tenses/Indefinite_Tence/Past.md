
## Pehchaan (Identification)

Jab Urdu mein sentence ke aakhir mein yeh alfaaz aayein:

**tha, thi, thay, ya, yi, yay**

Toh woh **Past Indefinite Tense** hota hai.

Jaise: khaya, gaya, kiya, likha, padha, soya — yeh sab past indefinite hain.

## Structure

**Positive:** Subject + V2 (second form of verb) + Object
- Mein ne khana khaya → I ate food.
- Woh school gaya → He went to school.

**Negative:** Subject + did not + V1 (first form!) + Object
- Mein ne khana nahi khaya → I did not eat food.
- Woh school nahi gaya → He did not go to school.

**Question:** Did + Subject + V1 + Object?
- Kya tum ne khana khaya? → Did you eat food?
- Kya woh school gaya? → Did he go to school?

## Zaroori Rule ⚠️

Negative aur Question mein hamesha **V1** lagti hai, **V2 nahi**, kyunki "did" already past ka kaam kar raha hota hai.

### Important NOTE: 
Ah, brilliant question! You're really thinking carefully about tense now! 👏

Here's the key: When you say "I went outside **to do** exercise," the "to do" is showing the **purpose** of going outside. It's not a separate action that happened — it's the reason for going.

Think of it this way:

- "I went outside" = past action ✅
- "to do exercise" = the purpose (infinitive form, always V1)

So the structure is: **Past verb + to + V1 (infinitive)**

More examples:

- I went to the shop **to buy** milk.
- She called me **to tell** the news.
- He came early **to help** us.

In all these, the second verb stays as V1 because it's showing purpose, not a completed action.

Does that make sense? The first verb is past, but the "to do" part always uses the base form!
