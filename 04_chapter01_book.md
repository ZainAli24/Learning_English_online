# 2. Synonyms & Antonyms:

## Synonyms (سائنونمز)

**Matlab:** Aise words jin ka matlab ek jaisa ya bohot milta julta ho.

Urdu mein hum ise **"hum-maani alfaaz"** kehte hain — yani same meaning wale words.

**Misaal (Example):**
- **Big** aur **Large** — dono ka matlab "bara" hai
- **Happy** aur **Glad** — dono ka matlab "khush" hai
- **Small** aur **Little** — dono ka matlab "chhota" hai

To agar aap kahein *"I have a big house"* ya *"I have a large house"* — dono ka matlab aik hi hai.

Yaad rakhne ka tareeqa: **S**ynonym mein **S** hai — **S**ame meaning (aik jaisa matlab).

---

## Antonyms (اینٹونمز)

**Matlab:** Aise words jin ka matlab ek doosre ke **ulat (opposite)** ho.

Urdu mein hum ise **"mutazaad alfaaz"** kehte hain — yani opposite meaning wale words.

**Misaal (Example):**
- **Big** ka antonym **Small** hai (bara ↔ chhota)
- **Happy** ka antonym **Sad** hai (khush ↔ udaas)
- **Hot** ka antonym **Cold** hai (garam ↔ thanda)
- **Day** ka antonym **Night** hai (din ↔ raat)

Yaad rakhne ka tareeqa: **A**ntonym mein **A** hai — sochiye **A**gainst (khilaaf, ulat).

---

# 4. ST. Nicholas translation Assessment:
Mashallah, aap ne bohot mehnat se translation ki hai! Overall aap ne **story ka matlab 80% sahi samjha hai**. Bas kuch jagah chhoti chhoti ghaltiyaan hain jo main ab ek ek kar ke samjhaata hoon. Har paragraph ke liye pehle aap ki translation, phir ghaltiyaan, phir sahi version.

## 📖 Paragraph 1

**English:** *At Christmas, children wait for St. Nicholas to bring gifts down the chimney. But it's not just a story. St. Nicholas was a real person.*

**Aap ki translation:** "Christmas ke waqt children wait kar rahe tha... St Nicholas ik real person hai."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ❌ "wait kar rahe tha" → Yeh past continuous hai. English mein *"wait"* hai (present), yani har saal Christmas par bachay intezaar **karte hain** — yeh aaj bhi hota hai.
- ❌ "real person **hai**" → English mein *"WAS"* likha hai (past tense). St. Nicholas ab zinda nahi hain, is liye **"thay"** aayega.

**✅ Sahi:** "Christmas ke waqt, bachay intezaar **karte hain** ke St. Nicholas chimney se gift laayenge. Lekin yeh sirf ek kahani nahi hai. St. Nicholas ek asli insaan **thay**."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 2

**English:** *A long time ago, a man named Marcus occupied a house with his family. He was not modest. He always told everybody he was the strongest man in the province.*

**Aap ki translation:** "...Marcus name ke ik admi ne ghar **fill kiya - gaheera** - apni family ke sath. wo koe **humble naram dil** nahe tha..."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ❌ "ghar fill kiya / gehra kiya" → Yahan **occupy** ka matlab "rehna" hai, "bharna" nahi. Yaad hai humne seekha tha ke occupy = *kisi jagah rehna ya qabza karna*? Yahan matlab hai "ghar mein **rehta tha**."
- ❌ "humble naram dil nahi tha" → English mein **"not modest"** hai, humble nahi. Aur modest ka matlab *"khud ko bara na samjhna"* hai, "naram dil" nahi. Marcus ghamandi tha.
- ❌ "taqatwar admi **hai**" → past tense: "**tha**"

**✅ Sahi:** "Bohot arsa pehle, Marcus naam ka ek aadmi apne ghar mein family ke saath **rehta tha**. Wo **modest nahi tha** (yani khud ko bara samajhta tha). Wo hamesha sab ko bataata **tha** ke wo soobe ka sab se taaqatwar aadmi **hai**."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 3

**English:** *He worked hard, but he could barely sustain his family. He wanted to save money and prosper. Still, he could never earn a penny more than he needed.*

**Aap ki translation:** "wo mehnat bhot karta lekin mushkil se apni family ka expense pora karta, wo chahta tha ke mien money ko save karo aur **khush raho**..."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ✅ Pehla hissa bilkul sahi hai! "mushkil se family ka expense pora karta" = perfect translation of *barely sustain*.
- ❌ "khush raho" → **Prosper** ka matlab *"khush rehna"* nahi, balke **"kamyab hona / taraqqi karna / ameer hona"** hai. Yaad hai humne table mein seekha tha?

**✅ Sahi:** "Wo bohot mehnat karta tha, lekin mushkil se apni family ka kharcha pora karta tha. Wo paise bachana aur **taraqqi karna (prosper)** chahta tha. Phir bhi, wo apni zaroorat se ek penny zyada bhi kabhi nahi kama paata tha."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 4

**English:** *One day, Marcus made an agreement with a blacksmith... Marcus wanted to help him forge iron. The blacksmith agreed to compensate him with a lot of money.*

**Aap ki translation:** "...Marcus chahta tha ke woh lohee se mehnat karke cheezay bnae, phir lohaar ne mahida karliya ke is ke badle mien tumhe bhot zayada dolat du ga."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ❌ "Marcus chahta tha ke **woh** lohe se cheezein banae" → English mein hai *"Marcus wanted to **help him** forge iron"*. Yani Marcus lohaar ki **MADAD** karna chahta tha loha dhaalne mein. Aap ki translation mein "madad" ka lafz reh gaya.
- ✅ Baaki sab theek hai!

**✅ Sahi:** "Ek din, Marcus ne lohaar ke saath ek mahida (agreement) kiya. Lohaar ke paas bohot kaam tha, lekin wo akele nahi kar sakta tha. Marcus us ki **madad** karna chahta tha loha dhaalne mein. Lohaar is baat par raazi ho gaya ke wo us ko bohot saare paise **compensate** karega (dega)."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 5

**English:** *In the same town, there was a man named Nicholas. At an early age, Nicholas started preaching. But he also believed that he should be humble and charitable...*

**Aap ki translation:** "...is ne **kaam ummer mien** yani jaldi hi jawani ki age mien tableeg shuru kar di thi..."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ❌ "kaam umar" → Sahi lafz hai **"kam umar"** (chhoti umar mein). Lekin aap ne khud hi samjha diya "jaldi jawani" — matlab aap ko concept samjh aa gaya, bas spelling chhoti si.
- ✅ Baaki sab bilkul sahi hai! "sakoon deta hai" = satisfaction ka perfect translation.

**✅ Sahi:** "Isi shehar mein, Nicholas naam ka ek aadmi raha karta tha. **Kam umar** hi mein Nicholas ne tableegh karna shuru kar di thi. Lekin wo yeh bhi maanta tha ke usay **humble (aajiz)** aur **charitable (sakhi)** hona chahiye. Usay pata chala ke logon ki madad karna usay tableegh se bhi zyada **satisfaction (sakoon)** deta hai."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 6

**English:** *"I worked hard for him," Marcus said, "but a problem arose. Even though I worked for him, he didn't pay me."*

**Aap ki translation:** "...lekin ik probelm **hai** ke mien beshak us ke liye **kaam karta hu** lekin us ne mujhe pay nahe diye."

**Ghaltiyaan:**
- ❌ "problem hai" → *"arose"* past tense hai (arise ka past). Matlab: "ek masla **khara ho gaya / paida hua**."
- ❌ "kaam karta hu" (present) → past tense: "kaam **kiya tha**"

**✅ Sahi:** "Ek din, Nicholas ka Marcus se **saamna hua (encountered)**. Marcus ne Nicholas ko apne lohaar wale mahide ke baare mein bataya. Marcus bola, 'Main ne us ke liye bohot mehnat se kaam **kiya**, lekin ek masla **paida ho gaya**. Kaam karne ke bawajood us ne mujhe paise nahi diye.'"

---

## 📖 Paragraph 7 — ⚠️ Yahan 2 bari ghaltiyaan hain

**English:** *That night, he went back to Marcus's house. He brought a bag of gold. It exceeded the amount that Marcus needed...*

**Aap ki translation:** "...Nicholas marcus ke ghar ki **back side pe chal gia**... ye bag full tha **jitni marcus ko zarroat hai utne pese**..."

**Ghaltiyaan:**

**1.** ❌ "ghar ki **back side** pe gaya" → Yeh bari ghalti hai! English mein *"went back"* ka matlab **"wapas gaya"** hai, "peechli taraf" nahi. *"Back"* yahan direction nahi, "wapasi" hai.
- **Go back** = wapas jaana
- **Back side** = peechli taraf

**2.** ❌ "jitne paise zaroorat hai utne" → Bilkul ulat! Yaad hai **exceed** ka matlab "**had se zyada hona**"? English kehta hai *"It exceeded the amount that Marcus needed"* yani us mein Marcus ki zaroorat se **ZYADA** paise thay.

**✅ Sahi:** "Us raat, Nicholas **wapas** Marcus ke ghar gaya. Wo soone ka ek thaila laaya. Us mein Marcus ki zaroorat se **zyada (exceed)** paise thay. Nicholas ek **ladder (seerhi)** par charh gaya aur soone ka thaila **chimney** se neeche gira diya. Marcus ne apne **benefactor (mohsin)** ka shukriya ada kiya."

---

## 📖 Paragraph 8

**English:** *Soon, people found out about Nicholas's gift. He became well known and loved...*

### ⚠️ 5. Yahan Confusion Hai — Chaliye Asaan Tareeqe se Samjhate Hain

**Statement thi:** *"Marcus was compensated by the blacksmith for helping forge iron."*

**Urdu matlab:** "Marcus ko lohaar ne loha dhaalne mein madad karne ke badle **paise diye thay**."

**Aap ka jawab:** True ❌
**Sahi jawab:** **False** ✅

**Samajhne ka asaan tareeqa:**

Yaad karein **compensate** ka matlab kya tha? → *"Kisi ko kaam ke badle paise dena."*

Ab story mein dhyaan se dekhein — do cheezein hui thin:

1. **Lohaar ne WAADA kiya tha** paise dene ka → *"The blacksmith **agreed to** compensate him"* (agreed = raazi hua, promise kiya)
2. **Lekin asal mein paise diye nahi** → Marcus khud kehta hai: *"he **didn't pay** me"* (us ne mujhe paise nahi diye)

To question yeh nahi hai ke "kya waada hua tha?" — question yeh hai ke "**kya asal mein paise mile?**"

Jawab: **NAHI mile** → is liye statement **False** hai.

**Ek roz-marra ki misaal se samjhein:**
> Maan lein aap ke dost ne kaha: "Main tumhe kal 500 rupay dunga." Lekin agle din us ne paise nahi diye.
>
> Agar koi poochhe: *"Kya dost ne aap ko 500 rupay diye?"*
>
> Jawab hoga: **Nahi (False)** — kyunke sirf waada karna aur asal mein dena do alag cheezein hain.

Bas yahi baat Marcus aur lohaar ke saath hui. Lohaar ne **waada kiya** tha (agreed), lekin **compensate nahi kiya** (paise diye nahi). Is liye statement False hai.

**Yaad rakhne ka rule:**
> **"Agreed to compensate" ≠ "Compensated"**
> (Paise dene ka waada karna ≠ asal mein paise dena)

**6. True ✅** — Story mein hai: *"It exceeded the amount that Marcus needed"* — yani paise Marcus ki zaroorat se zyada thay. Perfect!

## 💡 Aaj ki Sab se Ahem Baat

English mein yeh difference bohot ahem hai:

| Phrase | Matlab |
|---|---|
| *agreed to pay* | paise dene par raazi hua (lekin abhi diye nahi) |
| *promised to pay* | paise dene ka waada kiya (lekin abhi diye nahi) |
| *paid* | asal mein paise de diye |
| *compensated* | asal mein mehnat ke paise de diye |

Jab bhi aap story parhein, dhyaan rakhein: **kya sirf waada hua, ya kaam bhi hua?**
