# "The Client Who Could Never Decide"


Client: Hey! I need an inventory management system for my wholesale business.
Me: Hey! Ok, you need an inventory management system for your wholesale business. Please tell me which type of inventory management system you actually need.
Client: I need an inventory management system where I can track stocks, manage orders, track monthly revenue and generate reports — so this type of system I need.
Me: Ok, I noted your requirements and the best tech stack we will use for your IMS is React.js for frontend, FastAPI for backend logic, and we will use PostgreSQL database to store orders and data.
Client: Ok — deadline is only one month. We need the final product within one month.
Me: Ok — our team will start working on it from today.
Client: (Next day) Client called again and told me we should use Vue.js, it is a better option for frontend design and UI.
Me: Ok, but React.js is a better option. It has a very big and mature community — it is a better option, and my team already started working on it.
Client: No, we should use Vue.js, it is a better option.
Me: Ok. I told my team to shift from React to Vue.js.
Client: (Third day) Client called again and told me we should use Node.js for backend logic.
Me: Ok, but on the basis of your requirements FastAPI is the best option — ok, as you say we shift to Node.js. (I told my team that the client changes his requirements again and again. Call it a day, stop working on this client's product until the client finalizes his requirements — and my team threw shade, they said what kind of client is this!)
Client: (Fourth day) Client called again and told me please don't mind, I think MongoDB is the best option for data storing and managing.
Me: I told him that PostgreSQL handles very large amounts of data easily. I also told him hold your horses — please spill the tea, who is suggesting you that this is better and this is not?
Client: I saw it in YouTube tutorials.
Me: I told him please keep an eye on what I'm explaining — I explained everything clearly using Excalidraw to catch his eye and finally he understood my explanation.
Client: Ok, you are right.
Me: Ok, your product will be built on time and it will be exactly as you wish. Take care, bye.
(This client went viral in our company and team because he saw youtube tutorials and changed his requirements it was a very funny thing.)
