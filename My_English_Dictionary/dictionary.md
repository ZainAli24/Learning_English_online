# My Personal English Dictionary

English Phrases & Vocabulary with Urdu Meanings

**Last Updated:** May 14, 2026 | **Total Words:** 25

---

## 1. Call it a day (آج کے لیے بس کرنا)

**Meaning:** Kaam rok dena ya band karna kyunke aaj ke liye itna kaafi hai. Yeh tab bola jaata hai jab aap thak jayein, ya lagay ke aaj bohot kaam kar liya. Iska matlab yeh NAHI ke kaam adhoora chhor diya — balke yeh ek samajhdari ka faisla hai ke ab aaram karo, baqi kal kar lena.

**Example:** My brother was carrying heavy packs of cotton. He was working continuously for 6 hours, so I told him to call it a day — we will carry the remaining packs tomorrow.

---

## 2. Catch someone's eye (کسی کی توجہ کھینچنا)

**Meaning:** Kisi ki tawajjuh apni taraf kheenchna ya kisi cheez ka itna acha hona ke dekhne wala us par nazar rok le. Yeh do tarah se use hota hai: (1) koi cheez aapki nazar mein aa jaye, aur (2) aap jaanbujh kar kisi ki attention hasil karein.

**Example:** I use Excalidraw to draw diagrams and the architecture of the product to catch the eye of my team.

---

## 3. Give someone a hand (کسی کی مدد کرنا)

**Meaning:** Kisi ki madad karna — chahe kaam mein madad ho, paise se madad ho, koi cheez samjhane mein madad ho, ya koi bhari cheez uthwane mein. Har tarah ki help ke liye yeh phrase use hota hai. Yeh "help" ka friendly tareeqa hai.

**Example:** My junior fellows usually need help with a project or any concept, and I always give them a hand.

---

## 4. Spill the tea (چھپی باتیں بتانا)

**Meaning:** Raaz ya chupi hui baatein batana. Jab koi aap se kuch chupa raha ho ya aap excited ho wo raaz jaanne mein, toh bolte hain "spill the tea!" Yahan "tea" chai nahi hai — yeh bol chal ka lafz hai raaz ki baatein ke liye.

**Example:** My brother had his first interview and I told him to spill the tea — what happened in the interview? Did you get selected?

---

## 5. Glow up (نکھار آنا)

**Meaning:** Kisi ke andar positive change aana — shakal mein ho ya personality mein. Jaise koi school mein introvert tha, kisi se baat nahi karta tha, lekin college ke baad full confident ho ke har kisi se baat karta hai — isi ko kehte hain ke wo "glow up" hua hai. Yeh hamesha acha change hota hai, bura nahi.

**Example:** She was very shy when she was in school, but in college she had a glow up — she is more confident and doesn't get nervous when talking to everyone.

---

## 6. Low-key / High-key (چپ کے / کھلے عام)

**Meaning:** Low-key ka matlab hai chup ke, khamoshi se — yani kisi ko zahir na hone dena ke aap kya chah rahe ho ya kya feel kar rahe ho. Aap ke andar kya hai kisi ko nahi pata. High-key iska bilkul ulta hai — sab ke saamne izhaar karna, chupana nahi chahte, sab ko batana.

**Example (Low-key):** I work hard but I don't show it to anyone in my family, so I am low-key about my hard work.

**Example (High-key):** But if I get full marks in an exam, I share my marks with everyone — so I am high-key about any excitement.

---

## 7. Grey area (غیر واضح بات)

**Meaning:** Jab koi cheez clear na ho — na bilkul sahi na bilkul galat. Jab kisi rule, faislay, ya situation ka koi clear jawab na ho, toh us ko grey area kehte hain. Grey matlab salaiti — na white na black.

**Example:** The client said he will pay in 15 days or 30 days — this is still a grey area, nothing is clear yet.

---

## 8. Let's circle back (بعد میں واپس آتے ہیں)

**Meaning:** Kisi baat pe abhi faisla na ho sake toh baad mein wapas aana us topic pe. Jab meeting mein koi cheez abhi solve nahi ho sakti, toh bolte hain "let's circle back" — yani chhoro abhi, baad mein dobara baat karte hain.

**Example:** We don't have the budget numbers ready yet — let's circle back to this next week.

---

## 9. Concrete solution (پکا حل)

**Meaning:** Aisa hal jo clear ho, practical ho, pakka ho — na ke koi andaza ya guess. Jaise concrete se mazboot building banti hai, waise hi concrete solution matlab solid aur real hal. Jab koi mushkil ho aur aap bolein "hawa mein baatein mat karo, koi pakka hal do" — yeh wohi hai.

**Example:** This bug keeps coming back — we need a concrete solution, not a temporary fix.

---

## 10. This idea will never fly (یہ کام نہیں کرے گا)

**Meaning:** Yeh idea kabhi kaamyaab nahi hoga, chalega nahi. Jaise parinda urta hai (fly karta hai), agar idea "fly" kare toh matlab kaamyaab ho. Lekin "will never fly" ka matlab hai ke yeh plan practical nahi hai, kaam nahi karega.

**Example:** The client wants us to build the complete app in 2 days — this idea will never fly, we need at least 2 weeks.

---

## 11. Ghost someone (بنا بتائے غائب ہو جانا)

**Meaning:** Kisi ko achanak jawab dena band kar dena, bina bataye gayab ho jana. Jaise koi aapka dost hai, roz baat hoti thi, aur achanak wo message ka reply nahi karta, call nahi uthata, bilkul khamosh ho gaya — bina koi wajah bataye. Us ne aapko "ghost" kar diya. Ghost matlab bhoot — yani wo insaan bhoot ki tarah gayab ho gaya aapki zindagi se.

**Example:** Mera dost tha, roz baat karte the. Ek din se usne reply karna band kar diya, koi wajah nahi btayi — he ghosted me.

---

## 12. Throw shade (گھما پھرا کے طعنہ مارنا)

**Meaning:** Kisi ko seedha bura na bolein lekin ghuma phira ke taana maarein ya neecha dikhayein. Yani direct insult nahi, lekin aisi baat karein ke saamne wale ko samajh aa jaye ke uski burai ki ja rahi hai. Jaise koi bole "bohat achi jacket hai, meri dadi ke paas bhi aisi thi" — yeh seedha bura nahi bola lekin taana maar diya.

**Example:** Meeting mein usne mujhe bola "acha aapne bhi code likha hai?" — he was throwing shade at my coding skills.

---

## 13. Go viral (تیزی سے پھیل جانا)

**Meaning:** Koi cheez internet pe itni tezi se phele ke lakhon log dekhein. Jaise aapne koi video banaya aur wo TikTok ya YouTube pe itna share hua ke hazaaron logon ne dekha — wo video "go viral" ho gayi. Viral matlab jaise virus phehlta hai — tezi se, har jagah. Yeh positive bhi ho sakta hai (funny video) aur negative bhi (koi scandal).

**Example:** Maine ek funny video banai thi, next day it went viral — everyone in my college was watching it.

---

## 14. Keep an eye on (نظر رکھنا)

**Meaning:** Kisi cheez ya kisi insaan pe dhyan rakhna, nigrani karna. Jaise aap bahar ja rahe ho aur apne chhote bhai ko bolte ho "mera phone dekh ke rakhna" — yeh wohi hai "keep an eye on my phone." Eye matlab aankh — yani apni aankh us pe rakho, dhyan do, khayal rakho. Yeh do tarah se use hota hai: (1) kisi cheez ki hifazat ke liye — "mera bag dekh ke rakhna", aur (2) kisi pe shak ho toh — "is bande pe nazar rakho."

**Example:** When I connected my phone to the charger, I told my brother to keep an eye on my phone please — I am going outside.

---

## 15. Take it easy (آرام سے لو، ٹینشن مت لو)

**Meaning:** Pareshan mat ho, tension mat lo, aram se karo. Jab koi bohat zyada stress mein ho ya ghabra raha ho, toh aap use bolte ho "take it easy" — yani bhai shaant ho ja, itna pressure mat le. Yeh do tarah se use hota hai: (1) kisi ko tasalli dena — "exam hai toh kya hua, take it easy", aur (2) alvida kehte waqt bhi bolte hain — "okay bye, take it easy!" matlab apna khayal rakhna.

**Example:** My friend was worrying about his exam the next day. I told him to take it easy — if you have worked hard, your exam will go well.

---

## 16. Hold your horses (صبر کرو، رکو ذرا)

**Meaning:** Sabr karo, jaldi mat karo, ruko thoda. Horses matlab ghode — jaise ghode ko lagaam se rokna. Jab koi bohat jaldi mein ho ya bina soche kuch karne lage, toh bolte hain "hold your horses!" — yani ruk bhai, pehle soch, phir kar. Yeh tab use hota hai jab koi bina poori baat sune action le raha ho.

**Example:** My friend found a bug in the code and he quickly tried to delete the entire code. I told him to hold his horses — read the code line by line, find the bug properly, then try to fix it without deleting the entire code.

---

## 17. From my perspective (میرے نقطہ نظر سے)

**Meaning:** "Mere nazariye se" ya "meri raaye mein". Jab aap office mein ya meeting mein apni raaye dena chahte ho lekin seedha nahi bolna chahte ke "yeh aisa hona chahiye" — toh aap politely bolte ho "from my perspective..." Yeh ek shareef aur professional tareeqa hai apni baat rakhne ka. Isse saamne wale ko lagta hai ke aap apni raaye de rahe ho, thop nahi rahe.

**Example:** From my perspective, using FastAPI is better than Node.js for this specific AI project.

---

## 18. To be honest (سچ کہوں تو)

**Meaning:** "Sach kahun toh". Jab aap koi aisi baat kehne lage jo shayad saamne wale ko pasand na aaye — koi complaint ho, koi feedback ho jo thoda kadwa ho — toh pehle "to be honest" bol do. Isse baat soft lagti hai. Jaise aap seedha nahi bolte "khana bohat bura tha" — balke bolte ho "to be honest, the food was not good" — yani sach kahun toh khana acha nahi tha.

**Example:** To be honest, I think the colors in this design need to change — they don't look professional.

---

## 19. If you ask me (اگر آپ مجھ سے پوچھیں)

**Meaning:** "Agar mujhse puuchho toh". Yeh thoda friendly aur casual tareeqa hai apni raaye dene ka. Jab koi aap se mashwara le raha ho ya aap apni taraf se suggestion dena chahte ho, toh bolte ho "if you ask me..." Yeh "from my perspective" se thoda kam formal hai — dost, colleague, ya team ke saath use hota hai.

**Example:** If you ask me, we should start the meeting ten minutes early to finish on time.

---

## 20. Let me clarify (مجھے واضح کرنے دیں)

**Meaning:** "Mujhe saaf kar lene do" ya "mujhe wazahat karne dein". Jab koi galat faimi ho jaye, koi baat sahi se samjh na aaye, ya koi confusion ho — toh aap bolte ho "let me clarify." Yeh bohat zaroori phrase hai office mein kyunke client ya manager ko galat baat samajh aa jaye toh masla ho sakta hai. Yeh kehne se saamne wala ruk jaata hai aur dhyan se sunta hai.

**Example:** Let me clarify — the deadline is for the frontend only, not for the full product. The backend will be delivered next week.

---

## 21. Here's the thing (بات یہ ہے کہ)

**Meaning:** "Baat yeh hai ke" ya "asal masla yeh hai". Jab aap koi important baat kehne lage jo saamne wale ko shayad pata na ho, ya koi aisi baat jo poori situation badal de — toh pehle "here's the thing" bol do. Isse saamne wala samajh jaata hai ke ab koi zaroori baat aane wali hai, dhyan se suno.

**Example:** Here's the thing — this app needs a payment system, and that alone takes a week.

---

## 22. That reminds me of (مجھے یاد آیا)

**Meaning:** "Mujhe yaad aaya" ya "is se mujhe wo baat yaad aa gayi". Jab koi baat ho rahi ho aur achanak aapko koi purani baat, koi experience, ya koi cheez yaad aa jaye jo is se milti julti ho — toh bolte ho "that reminds me of..." Jaise dost koi kahani suna raha hai aur aapko apni koi milti julti kahani yaad aa jaye.

**Example:** That reminds me of our last project — the same thing happened and we were delayed by two weeks.

---

## 23. In my opinion (میری رائے میں)

**Meaning:** "Meri raaye mein". Yeh bilkul seedha tareeqa hai apni raaye dene ka. "From my perspective" se thoda kam formal hai — yeh roz marra baat cheet mein bhi use hota hai aur office mein bhi. Jab aap kisi cheez ke baare mein apna khayal batana chahte ho toh bolte ho "in my opinion..." Farq yaad rakho: "From my perspective" zyada formal hai (client/boss ke saath), "In my opinion" normal hai (team/dost ke saath), "If you ask me" sab se casual hai.

**Example:** In my opinion, React is better because it has a bigger community and more support.

---

## 24. Let me rephrase that (مجھے دوبارہ الفاظ بدل کر کہنے دو)

**Meaning:** "Mujhe dobara alag alfaaz mein kehne do". Jab aap koi baat kehte ho lekin saamne wale ko samajh nahi aati, ya aapko lagta hai ke aap ne sahi se nahi bola — toh bolte ho "let me rephrase that" aur phir wahi baat alag tareeqe se samjhate ho. Yeh "let me clarify" se thoda alag hai — clarify mein galat faimi door karte ho, rephrase mein apne hi alfaaz badal ke dubara bolte ho.

**Example:** The system will crash under load — let me rephrase that — the system needs optimization to handle more users smoothly.

---

## 25. I partly agree (میں کچھ حد تک متفق ہوں)

**Meaning:** "Mein kuch had tak manta hoon" ya "thoda agree karta hoon lekin poora nahi". Jab koi baat kare aur aap us se poora agree na karo lekin poora disagree bhi na karo — kuch hissa sahi lage kuch nahi — toh bolte ho "I partly agree." Yeh bohat achi professional skill hai kyunke aap seedha "nahi, galat ho" nahi bol rahe, balke respect se keh rahe ho ke kuch baat sahi hai lekin kuch nahi.

**Example:** I partly agree — we can extend the deadline by two days, but not a full week.
